Abstract

Real-time video denoising requires both high-fidelity recovery of fine spatial details and strong temporal coherence. However, existing methods have difficulty balancing reconstruction quality with computational efficiency. To address this challenge, RTDM is introduced as a novel framework that achieves state-of-the-art denoising performance while maintaining real-time inference capability. RTDM is designed by integrating three key components. First, a dual-path Mamba architecture is developed to decouple global spatial modeling from local spatio-temporal processing. The Global Mamba branch enables parallel sequence modeling with linear computational complexity, while the Hilbert-curve-scanned Mamba branch preserves local motion cues at minimal computational cost. Second, a progressive self-distillation strategy is adopted, where multi-level supervision is imposed using intermediate features and ground-truth signals through a spatio-temporal consistency loss. This strategy removes the need for external teacher networks, thereby avoiding additional parameters and computational overhead during both training and inference. Third, a bidirectional gated temporal fusion (Bi-GTFF) module is introduced to adaptively reweight cross-frame information via a learnable gating mechanism, which effectively suppresses temporal redundancy and ghosting artifacts. Extensive experiments conducted on a challenging benchmark with complex multi-source noise demonstrate that RTDM achieves 33.64 dB PSNR, outperforming the previous state of the art by 1.02 dB, while maintaining an ultra-low inference latency of 9.47 ms per frame. This simultaneous improvement in denoising quality and inference speed establishes a new performance benchmark for real-time video denoising and validates the effectiveness of the proposed design.

Progressive Self-distillation training framework

By taking multi-level features from the deeper layers of the network along with ground truth labels as supervisory signals, the framework globally and dynamically calibrates the feature extraction process through spatiotemporal consistency loss and multi-signal self-supervision, progressively distilling more refined spatiotemporal representations.

Visual comparison with other SOTA networks

The visual comparison highlights several common shortcomings of existing approaches in video reconstruction quality. PaCNet’s outputs suffer from pronounced over-smoothing, indicating a lack of high-frequency detail preservation. BSVD achieves reasonable overall noise suppression but leaves noticeable residual noise along edges, reflecting its limited edge-feature extraction capability. Basic VSR++, MMNet, and FloRNN each introduce characteristic artifacts, underscoring deficiencies in their temporal feature alignment and fusion. In contrast, our method—leveraging a carefully designed spatio-temporal feature extraction architecture and training strategy—delivers superior reconstructions: it effectively suppresses ghosting and other temporal artifacts while retaining rich texture details, yielding a more natural and visually faithful result.
